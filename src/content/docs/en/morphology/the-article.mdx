<Num index={chapter}/> For the meanings and uses of the article, →[{CH.S.ART}](../../syntax/the-article/). The article is invariant for case, number or gender, and only changes to suit the phonetic environment. The forms are as follows:

|	|	comment	|
|	----	|	----	|
|	*i*	|	before a consonant	|
|	*j*	|	before an open vowel	|
|	*ij*	|	before *e*	|
|	*in*	|	before *e*, *i*	|
|	*'n*	|	after *-e*, *-i*, *-ie*	|

<Num index={chapter}/> Examples:

+ <Tengwar tengwar=" ⸱" latin="Indis i·Ciryamo"/> — syllabic *i* between two consonants; 
+ <Tengwar tengwar=" ⸱" latin="auta i·lómë"/> — syllabic *i* after a vowel and before a consonant;
+ <Tengwar tengwar=" ⸱" latin="sac' i·fende"/> — syllabic *i* after *ë* and before a consonant;
+ <Tengwar tengwar="  ⸱ " latin="lírinen ómo j·aire táríva"/> — asyllabic *j* between two open vowels;
+ <Tengwar tengwar="  ⸱" latin="yassen tintilar ij·eleni"/> — epenthetic *j* before *e*;
+ <Tengwar tengwar=" ⸱" latin="coimas in Eldaron"/> — epenthetic *n* before *e*;
+ <Tengwar tengwar="  " latin="utúlie 'n·aure"/> — epenthetic *'n* after *ie*.

:::note
It is common not to spell epenthetic consonants, having *i* in every environment: <Tengwar tengwar="⸱" latin="i·indo"/>.
:::

<Num index={chapter}/> The forms of the article are prepositive (→ @@). For the purpose of accentuation they count as proclitics (→[{CH.P.ACC}](../../phonology/accentuation/#exceptions)).
