## Declension

### First-Declension Nouns

#### Stems and Types of First-Declension Nouns

<Num index={chapter}/> The first declension is also known as the **vocalic declension**, since it consists of nouns with a stem historically ending in an ā- and ō-sounds.

<Num index={chapter}/> The following types of noun belong to the first declension:

+ **strong**: *disyllabic* nouns or nouns with a *heavy penult*;
+ **weak**: nouns with a *light penult*;
+ nouns in <Tengwar tengwar="⸱" latin="-oa"/>.

### Second-Declension Nouns

#### Stems and Types of Second-Declension Nouns

<Num index={chapter}/> The second declension is also known as the **e-declension**, since it consists of nouns with a stem historically ending in an ē-sound. It is perhaps the most common declension.

<Num index={chapter}/> The following types of noun belong to the second declension:

+ **strong** nouns in <Tengwar tengwar="⸱" latin="-e"/>: *disyllabic* or with a *heavy penult*;
+ **strong** nouns in <Tengwar tengwar="⸱" latin="-ye"/>: *disyllabic* or with a *heavy penult*;
+ their **weak** variants with a *light penult*.

### Third-Declension Nouns

#### Stems and Types of Third-Declension Nouns

<Num index={chapter}/> The third declension is also known as the **semivocalic declension**, since it consists of nouns with a stem historically ending in short ĭ- and ŭ-sounds, syllabic or non-syllabic.

<Num index={chapter}/> The following types of noun belong to the third declension:

+ nouns in <Tengwar tengwar="⸱" latin="-ë"/>, which show <Tengwar tengwar="⸱⸱" latin="-i-"/> in inflection;
+ nouns in <Tengwar tengwar="⸱" latin="-ö"/>, which show <Tengwar tengwar="⸱⸱" latin="-u-"/> in inflection;
+ their **weak** variants.

### Fifth-Declension Nouns

#### Stems and Types of Fifth-Declension Nouns

<Num index={chapter}/> All fifth-declension nouns have a **stem ending in a consonant**.

<Num index={chapter}/> Although the endings used are generally the same throughout the entire declension (for exceptions, see the individual sections below), a number of different **subtypes** of the fifth declension are distinguished. The differences between these types largely depend on two factors:

+ (type of) the consonant in which the stem ends;
+ length of the vowel in the stem.

<Num index={chapter}/> It is often impossible to derive the stem of a fifth-declension noun (and thus the exact pattern of that noun's declension) from the *common singular* alone: the genitive is required to determine which subtype the noun belongs to. Compare, for example:

+ if *common singular* ends in <Tengwar tengwar="⸱" latin="-s"/>, the base might be <Tengwar tengwar="⸱⸱" latin="-ss-"/>, <Tengwar tengwar="⸱⸱" latin="-st-"/>, <Tengwar tengwar="⸱⸱" latin="-cs-"/>, <Tengwar tengwar="⸱⸱" latin="-ts-"/>, <Tengwar tengwar="⸱⸱" latin="-ps-"/>;
+ if *common singular* ends in <Tengwar tengwar="⸱" latin="-t"/>, the base might be <Tengwar tengwar="⸱⸱" latin="-c-"/>, <Tengwar tengwar="⸱⸱" latin="-p-"/>, <Tengwar tengwar="⸱⸱" latin="-ct-"/>;
+ if *common singular* ends in <Tengwar tengwar="⸱" latin="-r"/>, the base might be <Tengwar tengwar="⸱⸱" latin="-rd-"/>, <Tengwar tengwar="⸱⸱" latin="-rt-"/>;
+ if *common singular* ends in <Tengwar tengwar="⸱" latin="-l"/>, the base might be <Tengwar tengwar="⸱" latin="-ld-"/>.

:::note
In Late Quenya[^d53] final <Tengwar tengwar="⸱" latin="-t"/> became <Tengwar tengwar="⸱" latin="-s"/>, and subtypes (2) and (3) merged into one large group.
:::

##### Assimilated and Other Special Forms

<Num index={chapter}/> Nouns with simple bases have some distinct endings in the singular:

+ bases on <Tengwar tengwar="⸱" latin="-m"/> have **genitive-adjective** in <Tengwar tengwar="⸱" latin="-nwa"/>, **instrumental** in <Tengwar tengwar="⸱" latin="-mnen"/>;
