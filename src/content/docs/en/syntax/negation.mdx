## Standard negation

<Num index={chapter}/> Quenya has two main ways to form a negative clause:

+ **negation adverb** <Tengwar tengwar="" latin="ú"/>
+ **zero quantifiers**, such as <Tengwar tengwar="" latin="úquen"/> *nobody*, <Tengwar tengwar="" latin="úlume"/> *never*, etc (&rarr; [{`${CH.S.PRO}`}](../pronouns-and-quantifiers/#indefinite-pronouns)).

<Num index={chapter}/> Quenya differs somewhat from English in how these two methods interact with each other.

<Aside type="rule" title="Rule">If a zero quantifier is present, then it takes precedence over the negation adverb (&rarr; [PE23/99](../../reference/negation/)).</Aside>

1. <Tengwar tengwar="  " latin="úquen túle hai"/> 'nobody arrived on time'
2. <Tengwar tengwar="   " latin="ú melden úquen ambe lá·se"/> 'Túro didn't arrive on time'
3. <Tengwar tengwar=" " latin="cennenye·s úlume"/> 'I have never seen him'
4. <Tengwar tengwar=" " latin="cennenye úquen"/> 'I didn't see anyone' (lit. 'I saw nobody')
