## Subject – Finite Verb

### Basic Rule

<Aside type="rule" title="Rule 1">A finite verb agrees in **number** and **person** with its **subject**.</Aside>

<Aside type="rule" title="Rule 2">
Within a clause, **overt argument** noun phrases never appear with concording **personal endings**[^sfv1]

+ <Tengwar tengwar="⸱ " latin="i·cirya círa"/> *the ship is setting out* (overt third person singular)
+ <Tengwar tengwar="⸱ " latin="i·ciryar círar"/> *the ships are setting out* (overt third person plural)
+ <Tengwar tengwar="" latin="círante"/> *they (ships) are setting out* (implicit third person plural)
</Aside>

### Exceptions

<Num index={chapter}/> When the verb has a **covert object** and an **overt subject**, the objective inflection displaces agreement in number:

1. <Tengwar tengwar="⸱      " latin="sív' emme apsenet tien i úcarir emmen"/> \[VT43/12\]. *As we forgive those who trespass against us*.

<Num index={chapter}/> When a subject is **neutral group plural**, the verb can be singular:

2. <Tengwar tengwar="⸱  " latin="i·oromandi tanna lende"/> \[PE16/96\]. *The wood-spirits came thither*.

<Num index={chapter}/> The plural form of the verb may, however, be used to emphasize that the subject consists of various individual members:

3. <Tengwar tengwar="     " latin="Valar ar Maiar fantaner nassentar fanainen"/> \[PE17/174\]. *Valar and Maiar cloaked their true-being in veils*.

<Num index={chapter}/> When there are **multiple subjects**, the verb will normally be plural. Sometimes, however, especially when the verb precedes a first singular subject, it may be singular:

4. <Tengwar tengwar="  ​ ⸱  ⸱  ⸱ " latin="sanome tarne Olórin, Aracorno, Eomer, Imrahil"/> \[PE17/71\]. *There stood Gandalf, Aragorn, Eomer and Imrahil*.
5. <Tengwar tengwar="    ⸱ ⸱" latin="sinome maruvan ar hildinyar tenn' Ambar-metta"/> \[LotR/967\]. *In this place will I abide, and my heirs, unto the ending of the world*.

<Num index={chapter}/> The singular form may also be used with complex conjunctions, for examples <Tengwar tengwar=" ⸱⸱⸱ " latin="ú … ú"/>[^sfv2] *neither … nor*, particularly if nouns belong to the same grammatical gender:

6. <Tengwar tengwar="     " latin="ú luime ú lúme hora úquen"/> (translation from GN: GL/53). *Neither flood nor time waits for anyone*.

<Num index={chapter}/> The verb may agree in number with the subject in meaning rather than in grammatical form: this is called the **notional agreement** or **synesis**; for example, when the subject is singular but refers to a collective, the verb may be plural in form:

7. <Tengwar tengwar="  ⸱ " latin="lie tatallaner i·már ampanaina"/> \[VT49/43\]. *While it was being built people marveled at the house*. <Tengwar tengwar="" latin="tatallaner"/> agrees with singular <Tengwar tengwar="" latin="lie"/> in plural.

[^sfv1]: The only exception for the rule, <Tengwar tengwar="  " latin="esse úpa nás"/> seems to be an echo of archaic word order. Whether it actually belongs to Quenya proper is debatable.
[^sfv2]: Attested only in GN.

## Head – Modifier (in Noun Phrases); Predicative Modifiers and Complements

### Basic Rule

<Aside type="rule" title="Rule 1">An adjective, passive participle or adnominal pronoun agrees in **case** and **number** with the head it modifies.</Aside>

<Aside type="rule" title="Rule 2">Only one eligible component of a noun phrase receives the case inflection.</Aside>

+ <Tengwar tengwar="⸱ " latin="i·saila nér"/> *the wise man* (com. sg.)
+ <Tengwar tengwar="⸱ " latin="i·saile neri"/> *the wise men* (com. pl.)
+ <Tengwar tengwar="⸱ " latin="i·saile nerin"/> *to the wise men* (dat. pl. head-last)
+ <Tengwar tengwar="⸱ " latin="i·neri sailain"/> *to the wise men* (dat. pl. head-first)

:::note
Only some numerals decline: for indeclinable numerals, →@@.

Other types of modifiers do not agree with their head, particularly attributive genitive I, adverbs, cardinal numerals and prepositional phrases. For examples, →[{CH.S.SEN}.16](../simple-sentences#types-of-modifier).
:::

### Exceptions

<Num index={chapter}/> Modifier might not agree with its following head in **adverbial case**:

8. <Tengwar tengwar="      " latin="ono alye eterúna me illume ilya racsellor"/> \[VT44/9\]. *But deliver us always from all dangers*. <Tengwar tengwar="" latin="ilya"/> doesn’t agree with <Tengwar tengwar="" latin="racsellor"/> in number.
9. <Tengwar tengwar="    " latin="man tiruva cirya ondolisse morne"/> \[MC/222\]. *Who shall heed a ship on the dark rocks?* <Tengwar tengwar="" latin="morne"/> agrees with <Tengwar tengwar="" latin="ondolisse"/> in number.

<Num index={chapter}/> Note that if *modifier* receives adverbial case inflection instead, it **agrees in number** with its head as usual:

10. <Tengwar tengwar="    " latin="anar púrea ticta acsor ilcalannar"/> \[MC/222\]. *The sun blinking on the gleaming bones*.

<Num index={chapter}/> Modifier might not agree with its following head, if the noun phrase as a whole is a modifier in another noun phrase:

11. <Tengwar tengwar="  " latin="aran linta ciryalíva"/> \[PE17/147\]. *King of Swift Ships*.

<Num index={chapter}/> A demonstrative pronoun or a qualifier which serves as subject to a linking verb and predicative noun ('this is X') may either agree with that noun, or appear in the neuter:

12. <Tengwar tengwar="   " latin="ilya sí maller raicar"/> \[LR/47\]. *Now all roads are bent*.

<Num index={chapter}/> A noun in genitive II case usually behaves as an adjective, but might not agree with a head inflected for case or, in later use, if it takes on the role of genitive:

13. <Tengwar tengwar="   " latin="linte yuldar lisse miruvóreva"/> \[RGEO/58\]. *Swift draughts of the sweet mead*. <Tengwar tengwar="" latin="miruvóreva"/> doesn’t agree with <Tengwar tengwar="" latin="yuldar"/> in number.

## Antecedent &mdash; Relative Pronoun

### Basic Rule

<Aside type="rule" title="Rule 1">
A relative pronoun agrees in number and gender with its antecedent, but its case is determined by its syntactical function in the relative clause (for details, →@@).
</Aside>

+ <Tengwar tengwar="⸱  " latin="i·cirya ya círa"/> *the ship which is putting out to sea* (sg. inanim. as the antecedent, com. as subject in the relative clause).
+ <Tengwar tengwar="⸱   " latin="i·nér yen antanes miquelis"/> *the man to who she gave a kiss* (sg. anim. as the antecedent, dat. as indirect object in the relative clause).

### Exceptions

<Num index={chapter}/> The sense construction (→<a href={`#${chapter}.6`}>{chapter}.6</a>) is possible with relative pronouns.

<Num index={chapter}/> For other exceptions, especially 'relative repulsion', →@@.

## Apposition

<Num index={chapter}/> An appositive (→[{CH.S.SEN}.21](../simple-sentences#apposition)) **agrees in case** with the word it belongs to, but has **its own number and gender**:

14. <Tengwar tengwar="     " latin="Elainen tárin Periandion ar meldenya anyáran"/> \[VT49/40\]. *To Elaine, queen of Hobbits and my very old friend*.

<Num index={chapter}/> An appositive to a possessive clitic stands in the case of the host:

15. <Tengwar tengwar=" " latin="coarya Olwe"/> \[WJ/369\]. *The house of him, Olwe*.
16. <Tengwar tengwar=" ⸱   ⸱" latin="tintilar i·eleni ómaryo lírinen aire-tário"/> \[RGEO/59\]. *The stars twinkle in her voice’s song, of the holy queen*.
